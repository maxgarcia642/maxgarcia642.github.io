IPIP-300 · 300 items · facet-level readout

A Profile in
Five Dimensions

Most free tests hand back a four-letter type from a model researchers have largely abandoned — 16Personalities and other Myers-Briggs–style quizzes sort you into rigid boxes that shift on retest, while Enneagram and DISC lean on intuitive types with thin predictive backing. The IPIP-NEO — modeled on psychology's gold-standard NEO-PI-R — uses 300 items to resolve five validated traits into thirty facets, where shorter tests blur them into averages. The result isn't a label but a coordinate: a precise, shareable map of how one person thinks, decides, works, and holds up under pressure.

The sharpest signal in the profile — and the one most worth reading carefully. Five facets sit low, but one breaks the pattern: Altruism at the 61st. That single exception changes the whole interpretation.

Read the facets, not the headline. The instrument's "Morality" facet measures rule-conformity, and half its Agreeableness items really measure willingness to perform agreeableness. High Altruism beside low Cooperation, Modesty, and Trust doesn't describe someone who doesn't care — it describes a blunt helper: genuinely wants people better off, refuses to fake niceness, defer to convention, or trust on default. Synthesis 01 · what the combination means

The Rare
Combination

Individually, none of these scores is exotic. Stacked together, they describe a specific operating system — one that thrives in some environments and grinds against others. Here's the honest read of both.

Where it wins

High Assertiveness + Self-Efficacy + Achievement Striving + very high Openness + low Agreeableness is a founder/operator signature. Pushes ambitious ideas, operates independently, negotiates without flinching, moves before consensus forms.

Where it grinds

Low Dutifulness and Orderliness chafe against bureaucratic, rule-heavy, open-ended structures. The friction is never the technical work — it's deference, waiting, and doing things purely because you were told to.

How it executes

Builds its own systems rather than inheriting them. Disciplined on the craft that matters, loose everywhere else. Best pointed at problems where ownership and standards are self-set, not handed down.

How it thinks

Idea-driven and aesthetically wired, resilient under uncertainty, independent in belief. Comfortable with abstraction, novelty, and cutting-edge work most people find disorienting.

When two different instruments triangulate the same finding, believe it: the very low Cautiousness + high Immoderation + 90th-percentile Openness all point at one thing — the breadth is the superpower and the risk, and follow-through is the frontier.

Synthesis 02 · the profile against a life

Read Against
the Record

A personality profile is just potential until it meets a life. This one meets an unusually full one — IT and security work, a local-first AI ecosystem, progressive-rock guitar, a path toward the National Guard. The traits don't just describe the person; they predicted the path.

Genes are the start, not the verdict. The story is one of wills.

The forged facet

Extremely low Vulnerability isn't only temperament — it's the trait a hard stretch built and proved. The descent and the years climbing back out are exactly the kind of experience that pushes a facet to the 10th percentile and keeps it there. The test measured the thing the experience forged.

The discipline split, in the wild

388 tests and verbatim-exact identifiers on the craft; a string of institutional roles each lasting a few months. High Self-Discipline, low Dutifulness — the test and the résumé agree with each other.

The breadth, in the wild

Instruments (guitar, synths, drums, and starting a band), multi-agent AI software and workflows, robotics and drones, financial investment vehicles, networking hardware. 90th-percentile Openness isn't a hobby list — it's a structural trait expressed as a life.

On the path ahead: the most structured, highest-Dutifulness environment that exists meets a bottom-decile Dutifulness-and-Order profile. Not a reason to avoid it — a finite, structured tour can supply exactly what isn't generated internally. But go in clear-eyed: the friction won't be the technical work, which gets eaten alive. It'll be the deference and the waiting. Choose it with that loaded into the decision, not discovered six weeks in.

Closing · how to hold this

A Coordinate,
Not a Cage

Thirty facets make every detailed profile rare — rarity is a property of resolution, not of specialness, and it carries no verdict. What this readout offers isn't a destiny. It's a map of the terrain you're working with: where the wind is at your back, and where you'll have to row.

Lead with

The drive, the resilience, the idea-generation, the willingness to build and to lead. These are at the ceiling for a reason — point them at things worth building.

Build around

The two facets with the best evidence of rising through the twenties — Trust and Dutifulness — both run through everything you say you want: founding, family stewardship, community. The motivation's already installed via high Altruism. Only the habit is missing.

The one practical move the data hands you

This isn't only a self-portrait — it's a requirements document for the agents and collaborators you build. Most people configure tools in their own image and amplify their own blind spots. Spec at least one of yours as your inverse: high Cautiousness, high Orderliness, high Dutifulness — a finisher whose whole job is asking "did we plan this, is it organized, did we keep the promise" before anything ships. The 300 questions you answered just told you exactly which facets to install in your complements.

Find something to believe in, and find it for yourself. When you do, pass it on to the future.
